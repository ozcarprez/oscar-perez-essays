About the author

Óscar Pérez writes about the invisible connections in business — the patterns that cut across industries, the systems nobody questions, and the artificial friction that determines who wins and who loses.

With experience in agricultural export trade and fintech, his perspective comes from having operated on both sides of the table: the field that produces and the capital that finances. That intersection — where technology, regulation, and reality collide — is where his work lives.

Inspired by the thinking of Buffett, Munger, and Pabrai, his essays seek to answer a simple question: Why do things work the way they do, and what if they didn't have to?
